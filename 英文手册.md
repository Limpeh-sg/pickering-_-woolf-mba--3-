**Welcome Message from Dean**  
***Leading with Vision in a Changing World***  
* *  
  
The world no longer rewards leaders who simply respond to change. It rewards those who can anticipate it, interpret it, and act with conviction before others see where it is heading. In an era shaped by geopolitical uncertainty, shifting trade dynamics, political risk, and global disruption, the need for calm judgement, strategic foresight, and regional understanding has never been greater.  
The MBA at Pickering Global Campus is designed for a new generation of ambitious professionals, those who seek not only advancement, but distinction. Fully delivered online and shaped by an Asian perspective, the programme offers a premium and purposeful business education for young professionals and career switchers who are ready to lead with greater relevance in a changing world.  
As a member college of Woolf, Pickering Global Campus forms part of a pioneering global collegiate network that brings together academic rigour, international recognition, and technological innovation. This enables us to offer an MBA that is flexible in delivery, global in outlook, and rooted in the realities of Asia, one of the most influential and dynamic regions in the world economy.  
At Pickering, we believe that capable leadership is cultivated through reflection, preparation, and the courage to grow continuously. True progress is built not on complacency, but on the discipline to think ahead, the wisdom to prepare for uncertainty, and the openness to learn from diverse perspectives. These principles shape the character of our MBA and the leaders we seek to develop.  
This is more than a qualification. It is a platform to unlock potential, sharpen strategic judgement, and build the confidence to make decisions that matter.  
I invite you to begin that journey with Pickering Global Campus.

***European Union Recognition***  

|  |  |
| - | - |
|  |  |
  
* *  
All Woolf degrees are awarded with ECTS credits and are listed on the European Union Europass website. Transcripts are issued with Europass supplements to support academic and professional mobility across EU Member States. As the largest credit recognition framework in the world, the European Credit Transfer and Accumulation System (ECTS) allows students to earn credits throughout their studies that are recognised by hundreds of institutions and in numerous countries worldwide. This gives students the benefit of earning internationally recognised qualifications with credits that may be transferable.  
***ECTS Statement***  

|  |  |
| - | - |
|  |  |
  
* *  
ECTS standards, aligned with the European Standards and Guidelines 2015 (ESG 2015), are validated by an approved regulatory authority.  
The Malta Further and Higher Education Authority (MFHEA), partially funded by the European Commission and the European Union, implements ESG 2015 to promote quality across further and higher education. In September 2019, the MFHEA, formerly the National Commission for Further and Higher Education (NCFHE), licensed Woolf as an accredited higher education institution with degree-awarding powers. This licence is recognised within the European Qualifications Framework (EQF), supporting the portability of higher education credits and degrees across the European Union. Malta is also a member of the Bologna Process and the European Higher Education Area (EHEA). Programmes accredited through Woolf with ECTS recognition are clearly designated. As a recognised European higher education institution, Woolf is authorised to offer bachelor degree, master degree, and doctoral degrees with equivalent standing to those awarded by a university.

**MBA Modules**  
***Postgraduate Certificate in Business Administration***  
*10 core modules (30 ECTS)*  
##   
*Business Leadership and Strategy 3 ECTS*  
This module examines how leaders can use team strengths and organisational resources effectively to achieve business outcomes. Students develop leadership and managerial capabilities, with a focus on delegation, people management, engagement and strategic decision-making, and learn how strong leadership can drive performance beyond individual improvements alone.  
*Business Negotiations 3 ECTS*  
This module develops the knowledge and skills needed for effective negotiation in business settings. Students examine key negotiation theories, strategies and conflict resolution approaches, while practising through case studies, discussions and reflection. The module helps students build the confidence, judgement and personal style needed to achieve positive professional outcomes.  
*Ethical Issues for Managers 3 ECTS*  
This module explores the ethical issues and dilemmas faced by managers across organisations. Students examine ethical decision-making, responsible corporate conduct, the psychological dimensions of business ethics, and ethics in the global marketplace. The module develops the judgement and skills needed to lead responsibly and manage ethical issues effectively in practice.  
*Effective Managerial Communication 3 ECTS*  
This module develops the communication skills needed for effective leadership in business, professional and social settings. Students explore written, verbal and interpersonal communication, and learn to adapt their approach to different contexts, including informational, persuasive and relationship-building situations, while presenting themselves professionally.  
*Strategic Economics 3 ECTS*  
This module applies economic principles to managerial decision-making and analysis of the business environment. Students explore demand and supply, elasticity, market structures, pricing, firm behaviour, and key macroeconomic issues, while learning to interpret economic data and use economic reasoning to support strategic and operational decisions.  
*Marketing Strategy 3 ECTS*  
This module explores how marketing creates value for customers and organisations. Students examine the marketing environment, consumer behaviour, segmentation, targeting, positioning, and the marketing mix, including product, price, place and promotion. The module develops analytical and strategic skills for planning and evaluating marketing activity in competitive markets.  
*Supply Chain Management 3 ECTS*  
This module examines how organisations design, manage and improve operations and supply chains to deliver value efficiently and reliably. Students explore process design, capacity planning, inventory, quality, lean operations, coordination, risk and technology, project management while applying analytical tools to improve productivity, responsiveness and sustainability.  
*Technology and Operations Management 3 ECTS*  
This module develops the knowledge and skills needed to manage operations and use technology to strengthen competitive advantage. Students explore process analysis, product and service delivery, automation, cross-functional integration, product development, information technology, and operations strategy, with emphasis on improving performance across business functions and operating models.  
  
*Leading People, Teams and Organisations: Organisational Theory*  
*3 ECTS*  
This module explores behaviour in organisations and the influence of leadership on performance and change. Students examine motivation, conflict, team dynamics, culture, communication and leadership approaches in complex settings, while reflecting on their own leadership style. The module develops ethical, collaborative and effective leadership capabilities.  
*Creativity and Innovation 3 ECTS*  
* *  
This module develops the skills needed to generate creative solutions to business challenges and to lead innovation effectively. Students explore frameworks, methods and practical tools for fostering creativity, designing innovative responses and managing creative projects, while building the confidence to lead people, ideas and ventures in dynamic business settings.  
  
***Postgraduate Diploma in Business Administration***  
*3 core modules (30 ECTS)*  
##   
*Decision Making with Business Analytics*  
This module develops the analytical skills needed for data-informed managerial decision-making. Students explore business statistics, data visualisation, data communication, predictive analysis and modelling tools, while learning to interpret results, solve business problems and communicate insights clearly to support effective decisions.  
   
*Social Entrepreneurship*  
This module applies economic principles to managerial decision-making and analysis of the business environment. Students explore demand and supply, elasticity, market structures, pricing, firm behaviour, and key macroeconomic issues, while learning to interpret economic data and use economic reasoning to support strategic and operational decisions.  
*Financial Statement Analysis*  
This module develops the accounting and finance knowledge needed for effective managerial decision-making. Students examine financial statements, assess profitability, liquidity and solvency, and explore key corporate finance concepts such as capital budgeting, risk, return and the cost of capital, with emphasis on using financial information to support strategic decisions.
